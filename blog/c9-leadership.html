Leadership

What is your experience of compassionate (or not) leadership?

I've been fortunate/unfortunate enough to experience both compassionate and imcompassionate (sic?) leadership in the workplace.

I'll speak of the compassionate leadership, because I suspect most everyone has experience in the opposite. I've been very lucky to work in a place run by genuinely warm, compassionate leaders. I can honestly say that it inspired me to perform better (albeit not faultlessly) than I otherwise might have. When you consider your leaders to also be your friends, not only do you want to do well for them but - and perhaps even moreso - you find yourself struggling hard against letting them down, and being disappointed in yourself when you inevitably do.

Do the multiplying goodness meditation. How did you find it?

This was one of those exercises where I had some initial skepticism, but darned if I didn't really enjoy this one too. It was easy, and it felt surprisingly nice. I can truly see benefits in doing this every day.

Do the Tonglen meditation. How did you find it?

I found this one a little harder. Largely because I couldn't really home in on any targets with negative energy that I could visualise myself converting. I scraped through with some examples, but didn't really 'gel' with the concept as well as I feel I should have.

How does the SCARF model apply to your Phase 0 experience so far? How will it apply to bootcamp?

In a bid to make my life easier, I'm going to break SCARF down to best answer this question.

  • Status - I'd be lying if I said I haven't struggled with the idea of status during Phase 0. I feared I'd be languishing behind everyone else and be embarrassed by that. Fortunately my experience saved me from that fate. But then I sometimes found myself getting a little unnecessarily competitive. Where I wanted my projects to impress people more than others in the hope that I'd be the best in the cohort. To what end I don't know. I'm not proud of that feeling, and it's not something I want. It's certainly not how I feel most of the time, but I do have those feelings and thoughts.
  • Certainty - I have at times fought with the uncertainty of what is to come in the next sprint. I believe I've blogged earlier about my default position being one of panic. To automatically decide within myself that I can't do this. That it's too hard and I'm fooling myself. Despite the growing evidence (thus far) to the contrary, I still have that feeling though it does seem slightly more diluted than in the past. Slightly.
  • Autonomy - During Phase 0 I've done my best to counter stress by tacking the work head on. It's fair to say that Phase 0 has a wealth of glorious autonomy. I do have some anxiety about the same during Phase 1 since the schedule is more prescriptive.
  • Relatedness - I think, because we are all in this together and only for a relatively short time there's a great sense of camaraderie in our cohort. I've said it before and I'll say it again, we have a top-notch group of Hihi and I'm genuinely thrilled about that. So I truly feel that everyone is a friend. So far. Watch yourself ;-)
  • Fairness - Since we're all on the same curriculum it's certainly fair. I've felt a little guilty at times because I have more experience than others, but it hasn't taken long before we're all at pretty much the same point. Makes me wish I signed up for EDA months ago.

Have a difficult conversation. How did it go?

I hate this stuff, always have. No matter how delicately or diplomatically I approach difficult conversations, there's often something out of the blue that I didn't expect or anticipate. I'm a terrible debater. I'd generally just rather let the other person feel that they have the upper hand just so I can leave. It's an area of my life that could do with a helluva lot of polish. Actually, reading this chapter has inspired me to read that famous 'win friends and influence people' book. If it's not already obvious, I didn't have a difficult conversation with anyone.
