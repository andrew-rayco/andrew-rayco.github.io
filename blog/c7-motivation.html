Motivation

How does life look through the 'pleasure, passion and purpose' lens?

I'm going to be honest and say I don't really understand the question. How does life look? The 'pleasure, passion and higher purpose' honestly makes it look simple. Too simple? I don't know. Maybe things really are that simple and I just haven't figured that out yet.

It certainly made sense to me that we could more easily achieve sustainable happiness if we reverse the seemingly natural priorities of pleasure first, passion second and higher purpose last.

I understand that a strong focus on ones higher purpose is much more likely to make one happy. Not only do I understand the same about passion (see: flow), but I have signed up for EDA on this exact basis. I experience flow when coding more than anything else. So this concept felt very familiar to me when I learned of it in Sprint one or two. I think the difficulty for me (and I suspect most others) is knowing or discovering what your higher purpose is.

I was disappointed yet unsurprised to learn that pleasure from short term thrills only provides short term happiness.

From the 'discovering values and higher purpose' exercise, What are your core values?

You know, I really struggled with this. Many of these exercises have made me realise that I don't really know myself very well. I certainly don't have a very clear connection between my physical and emotional states. Or rather, I lack the ability to recognise them.

I've always felt strongly about the importance of being nice, so I guess that's a core value. I passionately feel that society has a duty to protect and care for those that cannot care for themselves, so naturally I find Trump's presidency difficult to deal with. I'm anything but selfless, but it pains me that so many are so comfortable looking after nobody but themselves and theirs.

I believe we have a responsibility to live more environmentally sustainable lifestyles, yet I'm happy to concede I could do much much more to do so myself.

What do you stand for?

I stand for kindness. Consideration of others. Smiling and saying hello at those you pass on a quiet street. I stand for people over profit. Honest debate. Local, national and global sustainability.

How did you find the resilience ideas and meditation exercise?

I've heard most if not all those resilience quotes from well-meaning OPs on reddit or facebook. I find they do motivate me and help to reassure myself when I'm feeling faily, but that reassurance is short-lived. Still, this sort of thing can only be positive if reinforced. I'm just glad the book didn't have those quotes laid over a picture of a beautiful sunset.

As alluded to above, I can't seem to easily recognise the connection between emotions and physical sensations, so I struggled with the exercise. I noticed no physical sensation when recalling either failure or success. It was interesting, however, to note that I found it all too easy to conjure up failure memories, but struggled to find any for success. That's the 3:1 negative:positive ratio doing it's thang right there. I am most willing and eager to learn optimism and unlearn pessimism, so that is something I'll be thinking about a lot when I'm feeling negative.
