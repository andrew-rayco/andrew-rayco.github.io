Self Confidence

What is the relationship between mindfulness and self awareness?

They are the same. It's an inner knowledge. You can't be mindful without being self-aware and vice-versa.

Try Body Scan and Journaling. What was your experience of it? How did you feel?

I really enjoyed the body scan. In the Headspace mediation sessions, we scan the body every time, but I always found it too difficult. Too broad. So I'd flitter all over just focusing on the standout areas. This method of the body scan was more specific. I learned something about myself today, that I really do better with pretty specific task requirements. I mean I guess I always had a strong dislike for ambiguity, so maybe I didn't learn it today, but also I feel like I kinda did? I don't know. This is weird, but great.

I tried the journaling. I've tried it before and it was very therapeutic for me when I broke up with my first love. I didn't have much success 'letting go' in the exercise today. It felt too forced and everything I wrote was surface-level dribble. Also, does it have to be handwriting? I'm a lot quicker if I type it (and it's actually legible). I get that it's more real and earthy and honest if it's handwritten.

What did you take away from the "My Emotions are Not Me" section?

To be honest it's not something I'd thought about before. To be sure, I tend to say "I am sad" as opposed to "my body is experiencing the phsysiological effects of sadness", so it's certainly a new concept to me and one that I'll take on board. I'm doing a lot of taking on board lately, and I'm really enjoying it. Really.
