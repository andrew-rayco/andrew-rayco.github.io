Self Control

When programming, what are some of your triggers? What causes a disproportionate emotional response?

I tend to overreact sometimes when I get a result that I REALLY wasn't expecting. If, for example, something works but in a completely different way to what I was hoping or planning.

I also feel a little triggered when I feel overwhelmed to the point where I don't know quite where to start.

How did you find the Siberian North Railroad exercise (Stop, Breathe, Notice, Reflect, Respond)?

I found this exercise very similar to the 'count to ten' method that I have used since my early teens. I had a temper tantrum that resulted in a bowl of Coco Pops flung against a wall, and I scared myself. I've not had an anger problem since. But as I read in Search Inside Yourself, attentional control alone is insufficient. I may need to try reframing and reinterpreting the meaning behind it all.
